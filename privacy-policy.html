Privacy Policy

This policy explains what information US Fellows receives through the website, why it is used, when it may be shared, and the choices available to you.

Effective July 3, 2026

ScopeInformationUseSharingRetentionYour choices
1. Scope and responsibility

A notice for website and program information

This policy applies to the US Fellows public website and to personal information submitted through website-based applications, nominations, host inquiries, opportunity proposals, or related Society processes that link to this policy. US Fellows is a national civic fellowship society owned and stewarded by US Council.

“Personal information” means information that identifies, relates to, describes, or can reasonably be connected with a person. This policy does not govern a third-party website, a Host Institution’s independent practices, or a separate activity that provides its own privacy notice.

By using the website or submitting information, you acknowledge the practices described here. The Terms of Service also apply to website use.

2. Information we collect

Information you provide and limited technical data

The information received depends on how you interact with the site. Reading public pages requires less information than submitting a fellowship application or institutional proposal.

Information you provide

  • Identity and contact details, such as name, email, phone, city, region, and country
  • Professional and educational history, capabilities, service record, and affiliations
  • Mission interests, proposed contribution, availability, and supporting statements
  • Nomination, reference, host, or opportunity information
  • Consent confirmations and communications with program administrators

Information generated through use

  • Basic request and security logs maintained by hosting or infrastructure providers
  • IP address, browser or device type, requested page, and request time where logged
  • Form submission status and technical error information
  • Records of review, correspondence, and administrative decisions
  • Information needed to detect abuse and maintain reliable service

Data minimization: Please provide only information relevant to the process. Do not submit government identifiers, financial account details, protected health information, or confidential third-party material unless an authorized US Fellows process specifically requests it.

3. How information is used

Program administration, communication, and institutional integrity

We use personal information to receive and evaluate applications and other submissions; assess eligibility, capabilities, mission alignment, and institutional fit; communicate about a submission; coordinate review; and maintain appropriate program records.

Information may also be used to develop a possible appointment or host relationship, confirm statements with permission or where otherwise appropriate, administer Society participation, support safeguarding and conduct processes, prevent fraud or misuse, secure the website, troubleshoot technical issues, and comply with legal obligations.

We may create aggregated or de-identified information for planning, quality improvement, and understanding program interest. We will not attempt to re-identify information treated as de-identified unless necessary to test the effectiveness of de-identification or permitted by law.

US Fellows does not sell personal information for money. The public site is not designed for cross-context behavioral advertising, and we do not use application content to build advertising profiles.

4. When information may be shared

Limited access for defined purposes

Information may be accessed by authorized US Fellows or US Council administrators, reviewers, advisors, and decision-makers who need it for the relevant process. Access should be proportionate to role and purpose.

We may use service providers for hosting, forms, workflow, records management, communications, security, and technical support. The fellowship application is designed to transmit applicant records to authorized program administration infrastructure using Microsoft services, including Dynamics 365 or related Microsoft cloud services. Those providers process data on behalf of their customers under their own service terms and security commitments.

If you are being considered for a defined host opportunity, relevant information may be shared with a prospective Host Institution or mission partner when appropriate to explore fit, coordinate participation, or protect involved parties. We will seek permission where the context requires it and will not treat an initial application as permission for unrestricted distribution.

Information may also be disclosed when reasonably necessary to comply with law or valid legal process; protect rights, safety, and security; investigate misconduct; enforce site terms; or support an organizational transaction or succession in which appropriate privacy protections continue to apply.

5. Hosting and external services

Infrastructure outside this website

The public site may be delivered through GitHub Pages or comparable static hosting. Hosting providers may log IP addresses and related request data for security and operational purposes. The site also requests web fonts from Google; loading those resources can cause a visitor’s browser to connect to Google’s servers.

Microsoft services may process form and administrative data in locations determined by the relevant service configuration and contractual arrangements. A link to another website, embedded resource, or service does not make its privacy practices part of this policy. Review the provider’s own notice when you use an external service.

The current public site does not set first-party advertising cookies. A third-party service requested by your browser may use its own technical mechanisms. If site analytics, optional cookies, or additional embedded services are introduced, this notice and any required consent controls should be updated before those tools are used.

6. Retention and security

Keeping information only as needed

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to administer the process for which it was collected, maintain legitimate Society and institutional records, resolve disputes, protect participants, enforce agreements, and satisfy legal, accounting, or security requirements. Different records may have different retention periods.

When information is no longer needed, we may delete it, de-identify it, or securely archive it where continued retention is required. Backup copies and records maintained by service providers may remain for a limited period under technical and contractual retention practices.

We use administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards intended to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure. No internet transmission or storage system is completely secure. Do not send sensitive information through an unapproved channel, and notify the Society through your established program communication channel if you believe information has been compromised.

7. Access, correction, and other choices

Requests about your information

You may ask to access, correct, update, or delete personal information associated with your submission. Depending on your location and applicable law, you may also have rights to receive a copy, object to or restrict certain processing, or withdraw consent where processing relies on consent.

Use the official communication channel included in your US Fellows correspondence or the Society process through which you submitted information. Describe the request and the submission it concerns. We may need to verify your identity and authority before acting. We may retain information when required by law, needed for security or record integrity, or otherwise permitted under applicable law.

You may stop a pending application or participation inquiry by notifying the responsible program channel. Withdrawal does not require deletion of every record and does not affect processing already completed before the request was received.

Do not submit a privacy request containing additional sensitive information. If an authorized agent makes a request for you, we may seek proof of authorization and verify your identity directly.

8. Children, international visitors, and updates

Additional considerations

The general website is intended for a broad public audience, but application and institutional submission processes are not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly solicit application information from children under 13 through the public forms. If you believe a child has submitted personal information, use an established Society communication channel so the situation can be reviewed.

Visitors may access the site from locations outside the United States. Information may be processed in the United States and other places where US Fellows, US Council, or their service providers operate. Those locations may have different data-protection rules from your place of residence.

We may update this policy as practices, programs, services, or legal requirements change. The effective date at the top identifies the current version. Material changes should be presented through an appropriate website or program notice.

Privacy questions: Use the official communication channel included in your application, nomination, host, opportunity, or other US Fellows correspondence. This avoids publishing an unverified contact address and helps route your request to the administrators responsible for the relevant record.

Become a Host Institution

Bring exceptional people into work that matters

US Fellows invites NGOs, research institutes, universities, civic institutions, and mission-driven organizations to become Host Institutions.

Hosts may propose public-interest projects, research opportunities, civic initiatives, advisory needs, field-aligned graduate appointments, or mission-based service opportunities.

Host Standards

Mission alignment and institutional responsibility

Each host relationship is built on public benefit, clarity, good supervision, and respect for the Fellow’s contribution.

  • The work advances a credible public-interest purpose.
  • The fellowship role is distinct, defined, and appropriately supported.
  • The institution maintains a safe, lawful, and respectful environment.
  • Expectations and feedback are communicated clearly.
  • The affiliation protects the integrity of the Society and the Fellow.

Before proposing an opportunity

Test whether the work is ready to host

A host proposal becomes credible when the institution can explain not only what it hopes to accomplish, but how a Fellow will be supported in accomplishing it.

There is a real owner

A designated person understands the institutional need, can make timely decisions, and has enough availability to guide the work.

The scope is consequential and bounded

The contribution is substantive enough to matter but focused enough to complete, review, and use within the proposed setting.

Resources match expectations

Data, tools, access, collaborators, safeguarding, workspace, and funding considerations are addressed before promises are made.

A reciprocal relationship

What hosts should expect from Fellows

Fellows are expected to learn the institutional context, honor lawful policies, communicate clearly, use entrusted access responsibly, and deliver work with rigor.

What Fellows should expect from hosts

Conditions for responsible contribution

Hosts provide role clarity, a respectful setting, appropriate supervision, feedback, and an honest account of constraints, resources, and intended use.

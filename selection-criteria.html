The Fellow standard

Qualities expected under responsibility

Selection considers the record, the person, and the contribution they are prepared to make.

01

A record with substance

Accomplishment may take many forms, but it should demonstrate capability, judgment, and sustained effort. In this context, consider excellence and capability.

02

Character under responsibility

Fellows are trusted with institutional relationships, communities, and the public meaning of the Society. In this context, consider integrity and character.

03

Readiness to contribute

Candidates should be able to explain what they can do, where it is relevant, and how they will approach service. In this context, consider service and mission alignment.

Becoming a Fellow

A whole-person assessment of merit and purpose

Selection considers accomplishment in context. Reviewers look for evidence of what a candidate can do, how they exercise responsibility, and why a particular mission or appointment makes sense.

No single credential substitutes for the full standard, and potential may be considered alongside an established record where evidence supports it.

Excellence and capability establishes the point of entry; contribution potential defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

From intent to practice

What the selection journey asks of you

The process is designed to understand the whole candidate: record, character, field, mission, and the practical contribution they are equipped to make.

01

Prepare

Gather a clear account of your work, capabilities, service, mission interests, and current readiness. Attend specifically to excellence and capability.

02

Apply or be nominated

Submit an expression of interest directly or through someone able to speak to your record and character. Attend specifically to integrity and character.

03

Review and conversation

The Society considers evidence, alignment, references where appropriate, and the conditions for a sound appointment. Attend specifically to service and mission alignment.

04

Appointment

Selected Fellows confirm expectations, host or project context, responsibilities, and the Code of Service. Attend specifically to contribution potential.

The candidate in full

What the selection journey seeks to understand

Selection considers accomplishment in context. Reviewers look for evidence of what a candidate can do, how they exercise responsibility, and why a particular mission or appointment makes sense.

01

Excellence and capability

Quality of work, depth of knowledge, disciplined execution, creativity, leadership, or demonstrated promise.

02

Integrity and character

Honesty, judgment, respect, accountability, humility, and conduct consistent with public trust.

03

Service and mission alignment

A credible relationship between the candidate's record, stated purpose, and the mission they seek to advance.

04

Contribution potential

Readiness, collaboration, field fit, and the likelihood of producing useful work in a qualified setting.

Begin with excellence and capability, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

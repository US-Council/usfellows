What is a fellowship?

A fellowship is a structured placement with a host organization — often unpaid, and often the first opportunity a candidate has to build a professional record in the United States. It is less about a single job and more about a relationship: one that can open doors to further study, work authorization, and long-term status.

A Foot in the Door

US Fellows specializes in unpaid fellowship placements built for candidates who don't yet have a US work history — the first credential that makes the next step possible.

Pathways

Three degrees, one shared shape

Whatever level you're starting from, the pathway follows the same general shape: education, a fellowship as an F-1 OPT (or J-1) fellow, and the doorways that open from there.

Bachelor's

Bachelor's → F-1 OPT Fellow

For candidates completing or holding an undergraduate degree, moving into a fellowship placement under F-1 Optional Practical Training.

Master's

Master's → F-1 OPT Fellow

For graduate-level candidates, a fellowship placement that builds directly on advanced coursework and specialization.

PhD

PhD → F-1 OPT Fellow

For doctoral candidates and graduates, a fellowship placement suited to research-driven and highly specialized work.

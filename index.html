What is a fellowship?

A fellowship is a structured placement with a host organization — often unpaid, and often the first opportunity a candidate has to build a professional record in the United States. It is less about a single job and more about a relationship: one that can open doors to further study, work authorization, and long-term status.

The Long View

Five phases, one journey

Every fellow's path looks a little different, but the shape tends to repeat. Here's the general arc US Fellows helps candidates plan for, start to finish.

  1. Arrival & Eligibility The journey can start with any foreign national who holds a completed high-school degree.
  2. Education A degree program — Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD — that leads toward a fellowship-eligible status.
  3. Fellowship Start: F-1 OPT or J-1 An unpaid fellowship placement with a partner organization, structured around F-1 Optional Practical Training or a J-1 exchange program.
  4. Career & Long-Term Doorways The professional record a fellowship builds can factor into doorways like EB-2 NIW or H-1B, depending on individual circumstances.
  5. A Lifelong Fellowship Fellows keep standing in USIVA's partner network long after a first placement ends — a relationship, not a one-time credential.

Long-Term Doorways

Where a fellowship can lead

A fellowship placement isn't just a line on a résumé — it postures you. Here are the doorways fellows most often ask USIVA about; none of this is legal advice, and every fellow's situation is individual.

F-1 OPT

The usual starting door for fellows who arrived on an F-1 visa — a chance to convert a completed degree into hands-on US experience.

J-1

The alternative starting door for candidates on the exchange-visitor track, supporting the same kind of early fellowship placement.

EB-2 NIW

A fellowship track record can help build the documented history this green-card category generally looks for.

H-1B

The professional record and network built during a fellowship are often what puts a candidate in front of a sponsoring employer.

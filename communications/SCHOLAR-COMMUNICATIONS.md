# R&D Scholar communications

This file is the operational source for the two communications designed with the Scholars Program. Public guidance is maintained in `international-rd-scholars.html` and the collaboration-protocol section of `scholars-network.html`. The International Scholar message is also implemented in the Azure Logic App.

## 1. International Scholar onboarding

**Subject:** International R&D Scholar Outreach, Thesis, and Funding-Opportunity Expectations

**Audience:** An International R&D Scholar applicant who separately opts in on the application.

**Trigger:** Send only after the Dynamics applicant Lead is created successfully, `fellowship_program` is `International R&D Scholar`, and `scholar_guidance_consent` is `yes`.

Dear [First name],

Thank you for applying to the US Fellows International R&D Scholar pathway.

An important part of participation is developing a credible research network around a clear thesis or technical question and identifying realistic funding or academic pathways. The goal is not to collect general endorsements. It is to help qualified people decide whether a specific, bounded contribution to your work is appropriate.

Before contacting a U.S. scientist, professor, or industry partner, prepare:

1. A concise thesis or project summary, research question, objectives, methods direction, and intended impact.
2. Your institution, degree context, and home advisor or professor.
3. Preferably, a written endorsement from that advisor.
4. Your CV, relevant publications, portfolio, and existing endorsements.
5. Several relevant solicitation identifiers or official opportunity links.
6. The proposed research or proposal network, clearly distinguishing confirmed participants from prospective contacts.
7. One specific request for each recipient.

A potential network may include your home advisor, a field-aligned research scientist, a U.S. higher-education professor or researcher, an eligible industry or mission partner, and other subject-matter experts required by the work. No person or organization may be listed as committed without explicit permission.

Review official opportunities through Grants.gov, SAM.gov, SBIR, STTR, fellowships, graduate assistantships, cooperative agreements, industry-sponsored research, and other relevant sources. Verify the research topic, deadline, applicant eligibility, citizenship restrictions, expected deliverables, data and intellectual-property terms, and eligible lead-organization rules in the official notice.

Make the request answerable. You may ask for thesis or methods feedback, an introduction, solicitation guidance, an endorsement, consideration of a subject-matter-expert or Key Personnel role, or consideration of a PI or Co-PI role where appropriate and institutionally permitted. A professor may also be asked about academic supervision, admission, a research or teaching assistantship, tuition support, or another graduate-funding route. These are separate decisions and processes.

Personalize each message after reviewing the recipient's expertise, publications, projects, institutional role, and relevance to your research. Do not send mass endorsement requests.

Never share classified, export-controlled, proprietary, unpublished sensitive, personally protected, or otherwise restricted information through a public form or unsolicited email. A conversation, referral, endorsement, or positive response does not imply supervision, proposal participation, employment, funding, sponsorship, graduate admission, an assistantship, laboratory access, legal responsibility, or institutional commitment.

Keep written records of endorsements, permissions, requested roles, and confirmed commitments. The complete guide is available at https://usfellows.org/international-rd-scholars.html.

US Fellows

## 2. Research scientist collaboration notice

**Subject:** International R&D Scholar Thesis and Research Collaboration Requests

**Audience:** A scientist whose professional contact information and potential participation have been approved for this purpose.

**Delivery:** Staff-controlled. Do not send from the prospect-triggered application workflow.

**Before sending:** Confirm the scientist's current professional email, institutional policy, willingness to receive requests, preferred subject areas, and any limits on use of their name or affiliation. Record that permission in the approved system of record.

Dear [Scientist name],

Selected incoming International R&D Scholars may contact you regarding thesis research, public funding opportunities, and the possible formation of a multidisciplinary research or proposal team. Your professional contact information should be shared only with Scholars whose proposed work reasonably aligns with the subject areas and participation boundaries you approved.

The Scholars are instructed to contact scientists selectively. Their goal is not merely a general endorsement. A proposed network may connect the Scholar and home-institution advisor, a field-aligned research scientist, a U.S. higher-education professor or researcher, an eligible industry or mission partner, and other expertise required by the work.

Whenever possible, the Scholar should already have discussed the thesis with a home advisor and should attach that advisor's endorsement. A complete request should include:

- A concise thesis or project summary, research question, objectives, and intended impact.
- The Scholar's institution, degree program, and home advisor.
- Existing endorsements or letters of support.
- A CV, publications, and relevant technical experience.
- Several official solicitation identifiers or funding-opportunity links.
- The proposed team and institutional structure.
- The exact role, feedback, referral, or decision being requested from you.

You may be asked for scientific feedback, methodology guidance, a limited endorsement, a referral, an introduction, solicitation guidance, or consideration of a subject-matter-expert, Key Personnel, PI, Co-PI, or proposal-team role where appropriate.

You are not obligated to respond or participate. You may decline, narrow the request, refer the Scholar elsewhere, ask for additional information, or request an introductory discussion with the Scholar, advisor, or proposed team.

A response, discussion, endorsement, or referral does not automatically commit you or your institution to thesis supervision, authorship, proposal participation, employment, compensation, funding, laboratory access, contractual responsibility, or legal responsibility. Any formal role requires separate institutional approval and must satisfy sponsor, eligibility, conflict-of-interest, security, export-control, intellectual-property, and contractual requirements.

No Scholar is authorized to list you or any organization in a proposal, team, endorsement, announcement, or public document without explicit permission.

Initial requests must not include classified, controlled, proprietary, export-controlled, personally sensitive, or otherwise restricted information. Please continue to follow all applicable employer and institutional requirements when reviewing or responding.

Thank you for supporting responsible research relationships and the development of emerging researchers.

US Fellows

## Ownership and review

- Program Operations owns recipient eligibility, opt-in records, and delivery monitoring.
- Scholar Program leadership owns content and policy.
- The approved Outlook mailbox owns automated sender identity and replies.
- Review both messages whenever program eligibility, privacy language, funding sources, application fields, or collaboration policy changes.
- Never place contact lists, applicant data, endorsement files, or connector credentials in this repository.

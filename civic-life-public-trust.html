A humanity-scale mission

Institutions worthy of participation and trust

Public trust is strengthened through competence, transparency, fairness, access, lawful conduct, and meaningful ways for people to participate.

Fellows can support the practical work of civic institutions without requiring partisan uniformity or treating trust as a communications problem alone.

Public access establishes the point of entry; trustworthy information defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

From intent to practice

From a large mission to useful work

Humanity-scale language must lead to specific contribution. Each appointment narrows a broad challenge into work a Fellow and host can pursue responsibly.

01

Understand the field

Map the challenge, affected communities, existing efforts, evidence, and important constraints. Attend specifically to public access.

02

Locate leverage

Identify a bounded question or institutional need where the Fellow's capabilities can matter. Attend specifically to institutional capacity.

03

Contribute with discipline

Work through a trusted host, respect domain knowledge, and produce useful, reviewable outputs. Attend specifically to civic participation.

04

Strengthen what endures

Share learning and reinforce the relationships, knowledge, and systems that carry the mission forward. Attend specifically to trustworthy information.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Listen before acting

Understand existing knowledge, lived experience, and institutional context before defining a response. In this context, consider public access.

02

Work across boundaries

Bring disciplines and sectors together when no single profession can address the whole challenge. In this context, consider institutional capacity.

03

Build durable capacity

Favor work that strengthens people, knowledge, and systems beyond the immediate intervention. In this context, consider civic participation.

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

Public trust is strengthened through competence, transparency, fairness, access, lawful conduct, and meaningful ways for people to participate.

01

Public access

Improve the clarity, usability, language access, and reach of civic information and services.

02

Institutional capacity

Strengthen operations, policy implementation, evaluation, legal access, and responsible administration.

03

Civic participation

Support informed engagement, community partnership, deliberation, and pathways into public life.

04

Trustworthy information

Advance evidence, public understanding, transparency, and resilience against confusion and manipulation.

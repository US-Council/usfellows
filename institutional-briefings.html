The Journal

Focused knowledge for consequential decisions

Institutional Briefings synthesize a defined question for leaders, hosts, advisors, and partners who need clarity without false simplicity.

They are designed for use: concise enough to engage, rigorous enough to trust, and explicit about assumptions, uncertainty, and implications.

The decision context establishes the point of entry; options and cautions defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Earn the reader's trust

Distinguish observation, evidence, interpretation, and recommendation with care. In this context, consider the decision context.

02

Write from substance

Ground contributions in credible inquiry, direct practice, or a clearly articulated argument. In this context, consider evidence and landscape.

03

Serve a larger conversation

Help Fellows, institutions, and the public understand a mission or act on it more responsibly. In this context, consider implications.

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

Institutional Briefings synthesize a defined question for leaders, hosts, advisors, and partners who need clarity without false simplicity.

01

The decision context

State the institutional question, audience, timeframe, stakes, and why attention is needed.

02

Evidence and landscape

Summarize relevant knowledge, actors, approaches, constraints, and points of disagreement.

03

Implications

Explain what the evidence may mean for policy, practice, partnership, capacity, or further inquiry.

04

Options and cautions

Offer practical considerations while distinguishing analysis from formal institutional advice.

Put this work into practice

Begin with the decision context, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

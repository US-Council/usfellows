R&D Scholar Standards and Standing

Every designation joins recognition to accurate eligibility, research integrity, responsible conduct, service, and continued accountability.

SelectionStandingIntegritySecurityPublicationServiceTitle use
Eligibility and selection

The whole record matters

Entry may occur through application, invitation, nomination, institutional partnership, or an approved research initiative. Selection is not based solely on academic credentials, job title, seniority, or years of experience.

Capability and evidence

Technical or scientific competence, research, publications, innovation, leadership, program delivery, and the evidence required by the specific designation.

Integrity and reliability

Ethical conduct, accountability, sound judgment, truthful representation, communication, and responsible handling of information and resources.

Mission and community

Public-service orientation, collaboration, mentorship, institutional contribution, and capacity to strengthen the US Fellows community.

Direct entry is possible. The designations are not a mandatory sequence. Each has its own gate, and admission or completion at one level never guarantees another.
Standing and continued recognition

Titles should describe current reality accurately

Active

The Scholar meets current eligibility and remains meaningfully engaged in qualifying work, program activity, or agreed service.

Inactive

A temporary status for career transition, leave, relocation, credential interruption, changed availability, or absence from qualifying work.

Alumni

Recognition of completed participation or prior active service without implying a current higher-level eligibility gate.

Emeritus

Selective recognition of an enduring senior record after active professional or organizational service has concluded.

Periodic review may consider current role, credentials, project participation, publications or technical contribution, mentorship, service, agreed support, conduct, and responsible title use. US Fellows may modify, suspend, or withdraw recognition through a fair, documented process appropriate to the circumstances.

Research integrity

Technical excellence cannot be separated from ethics

Scholars must communicate evidence, limitations, uncertainty, conflicts, and risk honestly. Fabrication, falsification, plagiarism, improper attribution, misrepresentation, undisclosed conflicts, misuse of funds, abusive conduct, retaliation, or circumvention of legal and institutional requirements are incompatible with good standing.

Responsible artificial intelligence

Tools support judgment; they do not replace it

Protect sensitive information, verify generated findings, disclose material use where required, evaluate bias and uncertainty, preserve human accountability, follow sponsor and employer rules, and never place restricted information in an unauthorized system.

Security, confidentiality, and sensitive work

Program membership never grants access

Access is controlled by law, contract, citizenship rules, institutional authorization, credentials, need to know, and the requirements of the particular work.

No implied access

The designation does not grant classified or controlled information, government systems, restricted facilities, sensitive research, proprietary data, export-controlled technology, or a clearance.

Authorized scope only

International and uncleared Scholars may participate only in work appropriate to their location, eligibility, authorization, applicable law, and project controls.

Safe verification

Public applications must never contain classified, controlled, proprietary, clearance-identifying, unpublished sensitive, or protected personal details.

Publications and intellectual contribution

Communicate work responsibly

Scholars are encouraged to publish, present, teach, and create technical resources when legally, ethically, contractually, and institutionally appropriate. Authorship and acknowledgment must reflect actual contribution.

Before public release

  • Obtain sponsor and institutional approval where required
  • Complete security, export-control, privacy, and intellectual-property review
  • Protect confidential and proprietary information
  • Disclose conflicts and affiliations accurately
  • Do not imply endorsement by US Fellows, a government entity, or another institution without authorization
Mentorship and service

Senior recognition carries greater responsibility

Service expectations increase with the seniority and distinction of the designation.

International

Learn, contribute responsibly, collaborate, and build credible research practice.

Resident

Honor structured commitments, apply mentorship, and develop dependable professional habits.

Trusted

Model judgment, contribute independently, and begin mentoring less-experienced Scholars.

Principal and Distinguished

Develop people, programs, partnerships, funding, standards, and long-term institutional capacity.

Mentorship must be professional. Exploitation, favoritism, harassment, coercion, inappropriate authorship, or pressure for endorsements, funding, access, or participation is prohibited.
Use of title and affiliation

Recognition must never be used to manufacture authority

Scholars may use a designation only while in good standing and according to program guidance. When relevant, active, inactive, alumni, or emeritus standing should be stated clearly.

  • Do not imply government employment, sponsorship, endorsement, or authority
  • Do not imply a clearance, access right, institutional role, or proposal commitment that does not exist
  • Do not endorse products, political positions, candidates, or fundraising on behalf of US Fellows without authorization
  • Do not use the title to solicit restricted information, facilities, funds, or preferential treatment
  • Distinguish personal views from those of employers, sponsors, government entities, and US Fellows
Suspension or revocation

Recognition is protected by accountable enforcement

Grounds may include material misrepresentation, research misconduct, serious professional or ethical violations, title misuse, unauthorized disclosure, relevant criminal conduct, loss of required eligibility, persistent failure to meet obligations, or conduct that substantially harms the organization or community. Where feasible, the individual should receive notice and an opportunity to respond.

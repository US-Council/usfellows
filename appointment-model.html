For Host Institutions

A shared structure for accountable contribution

The appointment model aligns four elements: a selected Fellow, a humanity-scale mission, a qualified host, and a defined contribution.

Clarity across those elements prevents fellowship from becoming an honorary label on one side or an unsupported work request on the other.

The Society approaches this work through defined responsibilities, capable partners, and standards proportionate to the trust involved. The aim is not activity for its own sake, but contribution that an institution can use and a Fellow can stand behind.

Guiding principles

What responsible participation requires

Different settings call for different methods, but these commitments remain constant.

01

A credible need

The opportunity should respond to real institutional or public-interest work rather than create an ornamental role.

02

Responsible support

A Fellow needs a designated supervisor, appropriate access, timely feedback, and a safe working setting.

03

Mutual value

The appointment should respect the Fellow's capabilities while producing useful value for the mission and institution.

In practice

Where the work takes shape

These areas illustrate the substance and range of contribution. Individual appointments remain focused, realistic, and matched to the Fellow and host.

The Fellow

Brings relevant capability, character, readiness, and commitment to the Code of Service.

The mission

Provides the public-interest reason for the appointment and the larger outcome it seeks to advance.

The host

Provides institutional setting, supervision, context, access, and responsibility for the opportunity.

The contribution

Defines the work, outputs, boundaries, learning, and intended benefit in practical terms.

A deliberate pathway

Building a strong host relationship

Good appointments begin before a Fellow arrives. Hosts clarify the work, establish support, and agree on what responsible completion looks like.

Propose

Describe the mission, institutional need, likely work, intended benefit, and responsible owner.

Qualify

Confirm organizational standing, supervision, resources, role clarity, and alignment with Society standards.

Match and shape

Align the opportunity with a Fellow's capabilities and turn it into a realistic appointment plan.

Support and close

Provide feedback throughout the work, review outcomes, and capture lessons at completion.

Quality over formula: The sequence can adapt to the field and institution. What should not disappear is clarity about purpose, responsibility, support, and learning.

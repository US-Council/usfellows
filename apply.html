The Conversation

What a Consult Covers

One conversation with USIVA touches four things.

Your Timeline

Where you're starting from today, and the realistic sequence of steps between here and a fellowship placement.

Your Pathway

Which pathway — Bachelor's, Master's, PhD, or the J-1 alternative — fits your degree and current status.

Your Paperwork Posture

The general categories of documentation your pathway calls for, and what puts you in the strongest position to move forward.

Your Doors

The longer-term doors a fellowship can open — from H-1B sponsorship to an EB-2 National Interest Waiver — and how a consult starts you toward them.

Step by Step

What a consult with USIVA looks like

  1. Initial conversation You tell USIVA where you're starting from — your degree or education level, your current status, and what you're hoping a fellowship could open up.
  2. Pathway fit USIVA maps your situation to a pathway — Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD into an F-1 OPT fellowship, or the J-1 alternative start.
  3. Partner match You're connected to the partner organization whose field fits best — Tao Learning, Curiosity Research Corporation, or INSTAR Lab Inc.
  4. Documentation & posture USIVA walks you through the general document types your pathway calls for, and what puts you in the strongest position to move forward.
  5. Fellowship & visa journey You move into the fellowship placement and the F-1 OPT or J-1 process that accompanies it — the first chapter of the longer journey USIVA maps out with you.
Get Ready

What to Prepare

You don't need a finished application before your first conversation — but having a few things ready helps USIVA give you sharper guidance.

Good to Have Ready

  • Resume or CV
  • Academic transcript
  • Proof of degree completion or current enrollment
  • A government-issued photo ID (passport)

Helpful If You Have It

  • Current visa or work-authorization documentation, if any
  • An offer or placement-interest letter
  • A portfolio or samples of your work
  • Publications or research output
  • Letters of recommendation

Don't have everything yet? That's normal — USIVA will tell you exactly what your pathway needs once your consult maps it out.

After Your Consult

What Happens After You Apply

  1. Confirmation You'll hear back to confirm your request has been received, generally within a couple of business days.
  2. Your consult A USIVA team member walks through your background, your goals, and any documents you've shared, and answers your questions.
  3. Pathway & timeline Together, you land on the pathway that fits and a realistic timeline for the steps ahead.
  4. Document readiness You start gathering what your pathway needs, with USIVA's guidance on what's required versus optional.
  5. Into the journey From there, you're moving into the fellowship placement itself — the opening chapter of the longer path US Fellows maps out with you. See what that path generally looks like in The Fellow's Journey.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Does starting a consult mean I'm submitting an application?

No. A consult is a conversation, not an application. Nothing is submitted through this site — when you're ready, USIVA's intake process happens through its secure external form at apply.usiva.org.

Is a consult legal advice, or a guarantee of anything?

No. US Fellows and USIVA are not a law firm, a consult is not legal advice, and no outcome — immigration, employment, or otherwise — is guaranteed. Immigration results depend on individual facts and circumstances, so always loop in a licensed immigration attorney for guidance specific to your situation.

How is my information handled?

Any documents or information you share as your process moves forward go through USIVA's secure case process — never public email or a public repository.

What if I'm not sure which pathway fits me?

That's exactly what the consult is for. USIVA will match your degree, status, and goals to the right pathway — Bachelor's, Master's, PhD, or the J-1 alternative. You can also compare them yourself on the Pathways page.

How long does the process take?

It depends on your pathway and individual circumstances, so there's no single answer. A consult is the fastest way to get a timeline that's actually specific to you, rather than a general estimate.

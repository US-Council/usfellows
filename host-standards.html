The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

Use these as a readiness review, not ceremonial language.

01

A credible need

The opportunity should respond to real institutional or public-interest work rather than create an ornamental role.

02

Responsible support

A Fellow needs a designated supervisor, appropriate access, timely feedback, and a safe working setting.

03

Mutual value

The appointment should respect the Fellow's capabilities while producing useful value for the mission and institution.

For Host Institutions

The conditions responsible contribution requires

Host standards protect the Fellow, the institution, affected communities, and the credibility of the appointment.

They establish a baseline while allowing the working model to fit different fields, from research and advisory projects to civic, educational, and field-based service.

Role clarity establishes the point of entry; responsible closeout defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

Host standards protect the Fellow, the institution, affected communities, and the credibility of the appointment.

01

Role clarity

Provide a written purpose, responsibilities, outputs, duration, working relationships, and known constraints.

02

Designated supervision

Name a person with the authority, availability, and subject context to guide and review the work.

03

Safe and respectful setting

Maintain appropriate policies, safeguarding, accessibility, and routes for raising concerns.

04

Responsible closeout

Review work, address entrusted materials, recognize contribution accurately, and report material issues.

From intent to practice

Building a strong host relationship

Good appointments begin before a Fellow arrives. Hosts clarify the work, establish support, and agree on what responsible completion looks like.

01

Propose

Describe the mission, institutional need, likely work, intended benefit, and responsible owner. Attend specifically to role clarity.

02

Qualify

Confirm organizational standing, supervision, resources, role clarity, and alignment with Society standards. Attend specifically to designated supervision.

03

Match and shape

Align the opportunity with a Fellow's capabilities and turn it into a realistic appointment plan. Attend specifically to safe and respectful setting.

04

Support and close

Provide feedback throughout the work, review outcomes, and capture lessons at completion. Attend specifically to responsible closeout.

04

Put this work into practice

Begin with role clarity, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

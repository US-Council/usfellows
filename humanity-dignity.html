Humanity-scale mission

Humanity & Human Dignity

Service focused on dignity, humanitarian work, immigrant communities, poverty, public health, access, civil society, and human flourishing.

A humanity-scale mission

Dignity as both purpose and method

Work in this mission begins with the inherent worth, agency, and knowledge of every person—not a view of communities as passive recipients of help.

Fellows may contribute through humanitarian, health, access, poverty, migration, rights, community, and human-flourishing initiatives led by credible institutions.

Humanitarian service establishes the point of entry; community strength defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

“Humanitarian service establishes the point of entry; community strength defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.”

The story of the work

Four chapters of contribution

01

Humanitarian service

Support responsible relief, recovery, coordination, protection, and locally grounded capacity.

02

Health and wellbeing

Advance access, public health, care systems, prevention, communication, and human security.

03

Access and opportunity

Address barriers to services, rights, education, participation, mobility, and economic possibility.

04

Community strength

Support civil society, trusted local institutions, inclusion, belonging, and long-term human flourishing.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Listen before acting

Understand existing knowledge, lived experience, and institutional context before defining a response. In this context, consider humanitarian service.

02

Work across boundaries

Bring disciplines and sectors together when no single profession can address the whole challenge. In this context, consider health and wellbeing.

03

Build durable capacity

Favor work that strengthens people, knowledge, and systems beyond the immediate intervention. In this context, consider access and opportunity.

From intent to practice

From a large mission to useful work

Humanity-scale language must lead to specific contribution. Each appointment narrows a broad challenge into work a Fellow and host can pursue responsibly.

01

Understand the field

Map the challenge, affected communities, existing efforts, evidence, and important constraints. Attend specifically to humanitarian service.

02

Locate leverage

Identify a bounded question or institutional need where the Fellow's capabilities can matter. Attend specifically to health and wellbeing.

03

Contribute with discipline

Work through a trusted host, respect domain knowledge, and produce useful, reviewable outputs. Attend specifically to access and opportunity.

04

Strengthen what endures

Share learning and reinforce the relationships, knowledge, and systems that carry the mission forward. Attend specifically to community strength.

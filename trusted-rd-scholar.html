Trusted R&D Scholar

Recognition for professionals who maintain an active U.S. Government clearance and serve full-time on projects, programs, or missions of U.S. national importance.

The qualifying gate

Both conditions must remain true

Trusted status is an eligibility-based professional designation, not a training completion award. A candidate must maintain an active U.S. Government security clearance and be actively engaged in a full-time professional capacity on work of national importance.

US Fellows collects only an applicant's confirmation through the public form. Clearance status, qualifying employment, and professional standing may be verified later through an approved, need-to-know process. Applicants must never submit clearance identifiers, sponsor details, program names, classified facts, or controlled information through the website.

Standard of trust

The title reflects judgment as well as technical ability

Trusted Scholars are expected to demonstrate discretion, reliability, professional accountability, and the discipline required in sensitive or mission-critical environments.

Integrity

Represent findings, qualifications, limitations, affiliations, and authority accurately.

Reliability

Meet commitments, maintain documentation, communicate risks early, and deliver quality work.

Discretion

Protect classified, controlled, proprietary, personal, and operationally sensitive information.

Judgment

Recognize limits, follow authorization boundaries, escalate appropriately, and preserve mission interests.

Professional contribution

Independent execution within defined authority

Research and analysis

Conduct independent technical work, experimentation, assessments, or development appropriate to the Scholar's role and authorization.

Workstream leadership

Lead defined tasks, coordinate dependencies, maintain standards, and contribute to reviews and planning with limited oversight.

Reports and proposals

Prepare defensible reports, briefings, publications, sponsor deliverables, and contributions to federal proposals or contracts.

Risk recognition

Identify technical, operational, ethical, compliance, and security risks and communicate them through authorized channels.

Cross-sector coordination

Work responsibly with government, industry, laboratories, universities, and other authorized mission partners.

Mentorship

Help Resident Scholars and less-experienced colleagues develop sound methods, documentation, communication, and professional judgment.

Security and compliance

The employing and sponsoring institutions remain in control

All access is determined by law, contract, citizenship requirements, sponsor policy, organizational approval, need to know, and the individual's current authorization. US Fellows does not validate sensitive eligibility through public materials and does not direct classified work.

Publications, presentations, mentoring, networking, and US Fellows activities must remain within all applicable prepublication, public-release, outside-activity, conflict-of-interest, security, intellectual-property, and export-control rules.

Protect affiliation boundaries

  • Do not identify protected programs, customers, facilities, or duties
  • Do not imply that US Fellows sponsors or controls qualifying government work
  • Do not use the title to request access, information, influence, or preferential treatment
  • Distinguish personal views from employer, sponsor, government, and US Fellows positions
Evidence and verification

Confirm the gate without exposing the work

A review may consider current role and organization, a non-sensitive description of the national mission category, professional references, public technical outputs, training, licenses or certifications, and confirmation of eligibility through an authorized security or personnel channel. The review should collect the minimum information necessary and should not duplicate an employer's or agency's security process.

A résumé, public profile, badge, business card, or personal statement is not clearance verification. US Fellows may defer, limit, or decline review when eligibility cannot be confirmed safely and proportionately.

Service without conflict

Contribute within employer and sponsor rules

Trusted Scholars may mentor Residents, review non-sensitive technical work, support approved proposals, teach responsible research practice, or advise public-interest initiatives. Any activity must comply with outside-employment, conflict, prepublication, ethics, time-charging, intellectual-property, and release requirements.

The designation never asks a Scholar to disclose protected work or use government relationships for private advantage. When an activity could create ambiguity, the Scholar should obtain the appropriate institutional guidance before participating.

Continued recognition

Trusted status is reviewed as circumstances change

Periodic review may confirm active clearance, qualifying full-time engagement, professional standing, ethical conduct, service, and responsible use of the title. Verification should be proportionate and handled through approved channels.

01

Active

Both eligibility conditions and program standards remain satisfied.

02

Transition

A lapse, suspension, expiration, or departure from qualifying work prompts review.

03

Inactive or alumni

Prior recognition may be retained without implying current Trusted eligibility.

Important boundary

The designation grants no clearance, access, employment, or authority

Trusted R&D Scholar does not grant or sponsor a security clearance; verify eligibility for government work; create employment, contract, proposal, or customer relationships; authorize access to systems, information, facilities, or programs; or confer government status. Those decisions remain solely with authorized agencies, employers, sponsors, and security officials.

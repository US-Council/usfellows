Mission in practice

The standard behind the distinction

Selection recognizes accomplishment, but the meaning of fellowship rests equally on character, discipline, public purpose, and conduct after appointment.

The standard protects Fellows, hosts, communities, and the credibility of the Society by making expectations visible from the beginning.

Excellence establishes the point of entry; responsibility defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

Selection recognizes accomplishment, but the meaning of fellowship rests equally on character, discipline, public purpose, and conduct after appointment.

01

Excellence

Bring demonstrated capability and a willingness to keep learning where the mission demands it.

02

Integrity

Represent work honestly, disclose limitations, honor commitments, and protect entrusted information.

03

Humility

Respect communities, colleagues, host knowledge, and the difference between expertise and omniscience.

04

Responsibility

Use fellowship recognition to strengthen the work, the institution, and the public good.

From intent to practice

How purpose becomes practice

The Society translates broad purpose into work that can be understood, supported, and evaluated.

01

Name the need

Define the public-interest challenge, its context, and the people or systems affected. Attend specifically to excellence.

02

Shape the contribution

Set a bounded role with clear responsibilities, useful outputs, and realistic scope. Attend specifically to integrity.

03

Support the work

Provide institutional context, supervision, access, and the conditions required for quality. Attend specifically to humility.

04

Carry learning forward

Document outcomes and lessons so one contribution can strengthen the next. Attend specifically to responsibility.

04

Put this work into practice

Begin with excellence, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

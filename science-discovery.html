The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Listen before acting

Understand existing knowledge, lived experience, and institutional context before defining a response. In this context, consider research and discovery.

02

Work across boundaries

Bring disciplines and sectors together when no single profession can address the whole challenge. In this context, consider scientific capacity.

03

Build durable capacity

Favor work that strengthens people, knowledge, and systems beyond the immediate intervention. In this context, consider translation and communication.

From intent to practice

From a large mission to useful work

Humanity-scale language must lead to specific contribution. Each appointment narrows a broad challenge into work a Fellow and host can pursue responsibly.

Understand the field

Map the challenge, affected communities, existing efforts, evidence, and important constraints. Here, attend to research and discovery: Support well-framed questions, rigorous methods, analysis, replication, and new knowledge.

Locate leverage

Identify a bounded question or institutional need where the Fellow's capabilities can matter. Here, attend to scientific capacity: Strengthen data, laboratories, research operations, open tools, workforce, and institutional systems.

Contribute with discipline

Work through a trusted host, respect domain knowledge, and produce useful, reviewable outputs. Here, attend to translation and communication: Make evidence understandable and useful without overstating certainty or erasing important limits.

Strengthen what endures

Share learning and reinforce the relationships, knowledge, and systems that carry the mission forward. Here, attend to science in public life: Help institutions and communities use evidence responsibly in decisions of public consequence.

Put this work into practice

Begin with research and discovery, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

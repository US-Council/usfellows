The Long View

The Fellow's Journey

A fellowship is rarely the whole story — it's usually the opening chapter. Here's the general shape of the journey US Fellows helps candidates plan for, from arrival through the doors a fellowship can eventually open.

1

Arrival & Eligibility

The journey can start with any foreign national who holds a completed high-school degree. That's the entry point US Fellows works from — we help you understand what a fellowship is and whether it fits where you're starting from.

2

Education

From that high-school foundation, most fellows move through a degree program — bachelor's, master's, or PhD — that becomes the academic basis for the fellowship placement that follows.

3

Fellowship Start: F-1 OPT or J-1

Most journeys begin their fellowship chapter as an F-1 OPT fellow — Optional Practical Training tied to the degree just completed. A J-1 fellowship is the alternative starting point for candidates on that visa track. Either way, this is usually the first placement that builds a US professional record.

4

Career & Long-Term Doorways

A fellowship well used becomes a foundation. From there, fellows and their host organizations often look toward longer-term status — pathways like EB-2 National Interest Waiver, H-1B, and other statuses that build on the record a fellowship established.

5

A Lifelong Fellowship

USIVA frames the fellowship relationship as something that doesn't end at the first placement. Fellows stay connected to the network of partner organizations well beyond their initial placement, as mentors, collaborators, and — eventually — hosts for the fellows who come after them.

Where does your journey start?

Whatever stage you're at — high school graduate, degree in hand, or already mid-fellowship — a conversation with USIVA is the fastest way to find your next step.

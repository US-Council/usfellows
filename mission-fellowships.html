Mission fellowships

Begin with the mission

A Mission Fellowship is structured around a defined area of public consequence, a capable Fellow, a qualified host, and an accountable contribution.

The fellowship pathway

Mission Fellowships are organized around a defined public-interest challenge rather than a conventional job category or a single professional discipline.

Appointments that begin with a consequential need

They are especially useful when the work requires cross-disciplinary thinking, a bounded project, or focused capacity inside a mission-driven institution.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Field alignment

A Fellow's knowledge and capabilities should be relevant to the work the appointment requires. In this context, consider research and synthesis.

02

Institutional fit

The host setting should make the contribution feasible, responsible, and useful. In this context, consider civic and institutional innovation.

03

Public value

The appointment should create benefit beyond private career advancement or organizational convenience. In this context, consider advisory contribution.

From intent to practice

From interest to appointment

Appointments are shaped deliberately so that recognition, institutional affiliation, and useful work reinforce one another.

01

Express interest

Describe your record, field, mission interests, and the contribution you are ready to make. Attend specifically to research and synthesis.

02

Establish fit

Consider merit, readiness, mission alignment, and the setting in which the work can succeed. Attend specifically to civic and institutional innovation.

03

Define the appointment

Confirm scope, host support, responsibilities, intended benefit, and a practical working cadence. Attend specifically to advisory contribution.

04

Serve and reflect

Complete the work with rigor, participate in Society life, and share transferable learning. Attend specifically to field-based service.

The story of the work

Four chapters of contribution

01

Research and synthesis

Clarify a question, gather evidence, map a field, or translate knowledge for institutional use.

02

Civic and institutional innovation

Design or test practical improvements to access, services, operations, or public engagement.

03

Advisory contribution

Bring experienced judgment to strategy, governance, technical questions, or mission development.

04

Field-based service

Support implementation, partnership, education, documentation, or learning close to the work itself.

Put this work into practice

Begin with research and synthesis, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

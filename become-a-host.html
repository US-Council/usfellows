From intent to practice

Building a strong host relationship

Good appointments begin before a Fellow arrives. Hosts clarify the work, establish support, and agree on what responsible completion looks like.

Propose

Describe the mission, institutional need, likely work, intended benefit, and responsible owner. Here, attend to define the need: Identify the institutional challenge, public context, and why a fellowship contribution is appropriate now.

Qualify

Confirm organizational standing, supervision, resources, role clarity, and alignment with Society standards. Here, attend to name the work: Describe core responsibilities, expected outputs, working relationships, and realistic boundaries.

Match and shape

Align the opportunity with a Fellow's capabilities and turn it into a realistic appointment plan. Here, attend to provide support: Assign a responsible supervisor and ensure access to the people, information, tools, and feedback required.

Support and close

Provide feedback throughout the work, review outcomes, and capture lessons at completion. Here, attend to plan for value: Clarify how the institution will use the work and what the Fellow should learn or carry forward.

Bring capable people into work that matters

Host Institutions turn mission into a setting where a Fellow can contribute with context, supervision, access, and accountable purpose.

The strongest proposals begin with a real need rather than a generic request for talent. They explain why the work matters, what success would make possible, and who will support it.

Define the need establishes the point of entry; plan for value defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

A credible need

The opportunity should respond to real institutional or public-interest work rather than create an ornamental role. In this context, consider define the need.

02

Responsible support

A Fellow needs a designated supervisor, appropriate access, timely feedback, and a safe working setting. In this context, consider name the work.

03

Mutual value

The appointment should respect the Fellow's capabilities while producing useful value for the mission and institution. In this context, consider provide support.

Put this work into practice

Begin with define the need, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

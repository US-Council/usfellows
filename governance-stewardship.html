Mission in practice

Accountability that protects the fellowship

Governance establishes how standards are maintained, appointments are reviewed, conflicts are addressed, and long-term institutional integrity is protected.

Stewardship is designed to preserve the meaning of the Society without making it rigid. Policies and decisions should remain responsive to evidence, mission context, and the people affected by the work.

Standards stewardship establishes the point of entry; long-term direction defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

From intent to practice

How purpose becomes practice

The Society translates broad purpose into work that can be understood, supported, and evaluated.

01

Name the need

Define the public-interest challenge, its context, and the people or systems affected. Attend specifically to standards stewardship.

02

Shape the contribution

Set a bounded role with clear responsibilities, useful outputs, and realistic scope. Attend specifically to appointment oversight.

03

Support the work

Provide institutional context, supervision, access, and the conditions required for quality. Attend specifically to responsible escalation.

04

Carry learning forward

Document outcomes and lessons so one contribution can strengthen the next. Attend specifically to long-term direction.

Rigor

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

Governance establishes how standards are maintained, appointments are reviewed, conflicts are addressed, and long-term institutional integrity is protected.

01

Standards stewardship

Maintain clear expectations for selection, appointment quality, conduct, and public representation.

02

Appointment oversight

Review host fit, mission alignment, role clarity, and appropriate support before affiliation.

03

Responsible escalation

Provide channels for concerns, conflicts, safeguarding, and questions about Society standards.

04

Long-term direction

Protect the public-benefit purpose and institutional continuity of the fellowship across cohorts.

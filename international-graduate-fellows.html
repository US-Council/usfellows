The fellowship pathway

Global perspective, field-aligned contribution

International graduates bring academic knowledge, cultural perspective, and connections across institutions and countries to American public-interest work.

Appointments are selected on merit and mission fit. They must remain grounded in a qualified host, clear responsibilities, and any independent legal or institutional requirements that apply.

Academic alignment establishes the point of entry; responsible participation defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

From intent to practice

From interest to appointment

Appointments are shaped deliberately so that recognition, institutional affiliation, and useful work reinforce one another.

Academic alignment establishes the point of entry; responsible participation defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

Express interest

Describe your record, field, mission interests, and the contribution you are ready to make. Here, attend to academic alignment: Connect advanced study to research, analysis, education, innovation, or other substantive field work.

Establish fit

Consider merit, readiness, mission alignment, and the setting in which the work can succeed. Here, attend to institutional contribution: Address a real need with outputs and responsibilities appropriate to the Fellow's preparation.

Define the appointment

Confirm scope, host support, responsibilities, intended benefit, and a practical working cadence. Here, attend to cross-cultural insight: Strengthen collaboration and understanding without reducing a Fellow to nationality or background.

Serve and reflect

Complete the work with rigor, participate in Society life, and share transferable learning. Here, attend to responsible participation: Keep fellowship selection distinct from immigration, employment, licensing, or academic authorization decisions.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

Use these as a readiness review, not ceremonial language.

01

Field alignment

A Fellow's knowledge and capabilities should be relevant to the work the appointment requires.

02

Institutional fit

The host setting should make the contribution feasible, responsible, and useful.

03

Public value

The appointment should create benefit beyond private career advancement or organizational convenience.

04

Put this work into practice

Begin with academic alignment, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

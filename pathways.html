Degree to Fellowship

Pathways

Every pathway shares the same shape: a degree, a fellowship as an F-1 OPT fellow, and the doors that open from there. Here's how that looks at the Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD level — plus the J-1 alternative for candidates already on that track.

Bachelor's

Bachelor's → F-1 OPT Fellow

For candidates completing or holding an undergraduate degree, ready to convert classroom learning into a first professional placement in the United States.

Who It Fits

  • Recent or upcoming bachelor's graduates on an F-1 visa
  • Candidates looking for their first US placement
  • Candidates who want a foundation before deciding on graduate study

General Paperwork Involved

  • Proof of degree completion or enrollment status
  • Academic and identity documentation
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements
  • OPT authorization filings tied to your F-1 status

What It Postures You For

A documented US professional record and a direct line into USIVA's partner network for the next placement or the next degree.

Doors It Opens

Graduate study, a second fellowship, or an early step toward longer-term status like H-1B.

Master's

Master's → F-1 OPT Fellow

For graduate-level candidates, a fellowship placement that builds directly on advanced coursework and specialization.

Who It Fits

  • Master's graduates and candidates on an F-1 visa
  • Candidates with a specialized field who want a placement matched to it
  • Candidates aiming toward research or technical roles

General Paperwork Involved

  • Proof of degree completion and field of study
  • Academic and identity documentation
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements
  • OPT authorization filings, including STEM extension where applicable

What It Postures You For

A specialized placement record that speaks directly to your field, plus a longer runway if your program qualifies for a STEM OPT extension.

Doors It Opens

H-1B sponsorship conversations, EB-2 NIW groundwork, or a doctoral pathway.

PhD

PhD → F-1 OPT Fellow

For doctoral candidates and graduates, a fellowship placement suited to research-driven and highly specialized work.

Who It Fits

  • Doctoral candidates and graduates on an F-1 visa
  • Researchers looking for a placement tied to their dissertation field
  • Candidates aiming toward long-term research or academic careers in the US

General Paperwork Involved

  • Proof of degree completion or candidacy
  • Research and publication documentation
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements
  • OPT authorization filings, including STEM extension where applicable

What It Postures You For

A research record built in the US, and the kind of documented national-interest contribution that later status doors often ask for.

Doors It Opens

EB-2 National Interest Waiver, research-track H-1B sponsorship, and continued fellowship relationships in your field.

J-1 Alternative

The J-1 Alternative Start

Not every fellow's journey starts on an F-1. For candidates already on a J-1 exchange visitor track, a J-1 fellowship is the alternative entry point into the same long-view journey.

Who It Fits

  • Candidates already holding or pursuing J-1 exchange visitor status
  • Candidates whose academic or professional exchange program supports a fellowship placement

General Paperwork Involved

  • J-1 program sponsorship documentation
  • Identity and exchange-visitor status documentation
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements

What It Postures You For

The same kind of documented US placement record as an F-1 OPT fellowship, reached through a different visa track.

Doors It Opens

A continued fellowship relationship, and — depending on individual circumstances — a later transition toward F-1-based or employment-based statuses.

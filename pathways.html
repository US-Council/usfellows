Degree to Fellowship

Pathways

Every US Fellows journey starts with a completed high-school degree. From there, three degree pathways — Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD — and one exchange pathway, the J-1, lead into a fellowship. Each shares the same underlying shape: a period of study, a status window that can support a fellowship placement, and the fellowship itself as an F-1 OPT or J-1 fellow. Below is how that looks at each level — who it tends to fit, how the journey unfolds, the general categories of paperwork involved, and the doors it typically opens.

Bachelor's

Bachelor's → F-1 OPT Fellow

For candidates completing or holding an undergraduate degree, ready to convert classroom learning into a first professional placement in the United States. This is typically the earliest point in a fellow's journey where a fellowship placement becomes possible.

Who It Fits

  • Recent or upcoming bachelor's graduates on an F-1 visa
  • Candidates looking for their first US placement and professional record
  • Candidates who want a foundation before deciding on graduate study
  • Candidates early in life who are still shaping their long-term field

The Journey

  1. Study Complete, or advance toward completing, a bachelor's degree — the academic foundation this whole pathway is built on.
  2. OPT Window Move into the F-1 Optional Practical Training window tied to that degree, the period during which OPT work authorization can support a placement.
  3. Fellowship Placement Step into a US Fellows placement as an F-1 OPT fellow, building a first documented professional record in the United States.

General Document Categories

  • Academic transcripts and proof of degree completion or expected completion
  • Program acceptance or enrollment verification, including status paperwork such as Form I-20
  • Identity documentation supporting F-1 status
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements
  • Financial documentation supporting the OPT period

What It Postures You For

A documented US professional record and a direct line into USIVA's partner network, whether the next step is another placement or graduate study.

Doors It Typically Opens

Graduate study, a second fellowship, or an early step toward longer-term status. See Long-Term Doors for how a placement like this can fit into the fuller Fellow's Journey .

Master's

Master's → F-1 OPT Fellow

For graduate-level candidates, a fellowship placement that builds directly on advanced coursework and a chosen specialization, rather than starting from a general foundation.

Who It Fits

  • Master's graduates and candidates on an F-1 visa
  • Candidates with a specialized field who want a placement matched to it
  • Candidates aiming toward research or technical roles
  • Candidates already weighing a longer-term US career path

The Journey

  1. Study Complete, or advance toward completing, a master's degree in a specialized field of study.
  2. OPT Window Move into the F-1 OPT window tied to that degree — for candidates in qualifying fields, this window can be extended under the STEM OPT extension category.
  3. Fellowship Placement Step into a specialized fellowship placement that speaks directly to your field of study, as an F-1 OPT fellow.

General Document Categories

  • Academic transcripts and proof of degree completion, plus field-of-study documentation
  • Program acceptance or enrollment verification, including status paperwork such as Form I-20
  • Identity documentation supporting F-1 status
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements
  • Documentation supporting a STEM OPT extension, where the field of study qualifies

What It Postures You For

A specialized placement record that speaks directly to your field, plus a longer runway if your program qualifies for a STEM OPT extension.

Doors It Typically Opens

H-1B sponsorship conversations, early EB-2 National Interest Waiver groundwork, or a doctoral pathway. See Long-Term Doors and the fuller Fellow's Journey .

PhD

PhD → F-1 OPT Fellow

For doctoral candidates and graduates, a fellowship placement suited to research-driven and highly specialized work, often continuing a line of research already underway.

Who It Fits

  • Doctoral candidates and graduates on an F-1 visa
  • Researchers looking for a placement tied to their dissertation field
  • Candidates aiming toward long-term research or academic careers in the US
  • Candidates who already have a body of published or documented research

The Journey

  1. Study Advance toward, or complete, doctoral candidacy — the research foundation the fellowship placement builds on.
  2. OPT Window Move into the F-1 OPT window tied to the doctoral degree, with the same potential for a STEM OPT extension in qualifying research fields.
  3. Fellowship Placement Step into a research-oriented fellowship placement as an F-1 OPT fellow, extending your dissertation field into applied work.

General Document Categories

  • Proof of degree completion or doctoral candidacy status
  • Research and publication documentation tied to your field
  • Status paperwork supporting F-1 standing, including Form I-20
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements
  • Documentation supporting a STEM OPT extension, where the field of study qualifies

What It Postures You For

A research record built in the United States, and the kind of documented, field-specific contribution that later status doors often ask candidates to demonstrate.

Doors It Typically Opens

EB-2 National Interest Waiver groundwork, research-track H-1B sponsorship conversations, and continued fellowship relationships in your field. See Long-Term Doors and the fuller Fellow's Journey .

J-1 Alternative

The J-1 Alternative Start

Not every fellow's journey starts on an F-1. For candidates already on a J-1 exchange visitor track, a J-1 fellowship is the alternative entry point into the same long-view journey — reached through a different visa category rather than a degree-plus-OPT sequence.

Who It Fits

  • Candidates already holding or pursuing J-1 exchange visitor status
  • Candidates whose academic or professional exchange program supports a fellowship-type placement
  • Candidates whose path did not run through a US bachelor's, master's, or PhD program

The Journey

  1. Exchange Status Enter or hold J-1 exchange visitor status through a qualifying academic or professional exchange program.
  2. Program Fit Confirm that the exchange program's category and sponsor support a fellowship-type placement under J-1 rules.
  3. Fellowship Placement Step into a US Fellows placement as a J-1 fellow — the same kind of first US placement record, reached through a different visa track.

General Document Categories

  • J-1 program sponsorship documentation, including status paperwork such as Form DS-2019
  • Identity documentation supporting exchange-visitor status
  • Fellowship placement and host-organization agreements

What It Postures You For

The same kind of documented US placement record as an F-1 OPT fellowship, reached through a different visa track.

Doors It Typically Opens

A continued fellowship relationship, and — depending on individual circumstances — a later transition toward F-1-based or employment-based statuses. See Long-Term Doors and the fuller Fellow's Journey .

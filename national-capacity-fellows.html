From intent to practice

From interest to appointment

Appointments are shaped deliberately so that recognition, institutional affiliation, and useful work reinforce one another.

Express interest

Describe your record, field, mission interests, and the contribution you are ready to make. Here, attend to infrastructure: Support the reliability, modernization, and responsible management of essential physical systems.

Establish fit

Consider merit, readiness, mission alignment, and the setting in which the work can succeed. Here, attend to cybersecurity and technology: Improve digital resilience, security practice, public-interest technology, and institutional preparedness.

Define the appointment

Confirm scope, host support, responsibilities, intended benefit, and a practical working cadence. Here, attend to emergency readiness: Strengthen planning, coordination, continuity, risk analysis, and learning before and after crises.

Serve and reflect

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Field alignment

A Fellow's knowledge and capabilities should be relevant to the work the appointment requires. In this context, consider infrastructure.

02

Institutional fit

The host setting should make the contribution feasible, responsible, and useful. In this context, consider cybersecurity and technology.

03

Public value

The appointment should create benefit beyond private career advancement or organizational convenience. In this context, consider emergency readiness.

Put this work into practice

Begin with infrastructure, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

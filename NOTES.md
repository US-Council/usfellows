# Vision Statement

US Fellows envisions a nation where exceptional people from every discipline can dedicate their knowledge, talent, imagination, and service to the great public missions of our time.

---

# Why US Fellows Exists

The most urgent challenges of our time require more than institutions, funding, and ideas. They require exceptional people willing to serve.

Across the United States and around the world, mission-driven organizations are working on problems of enormous public consequence: scientific discovery, human dignity, civic trust, climate resilience, education, immigration, technology, public health, humanitarian service, and national capacity.

US Fellows exists to create a distinguished civic pathway for people who want to contribute their abilities to these missions with seriousness, honor, and institutional support.

US Fellows is not a staffing agency, job-placement service, or training company. It is a fellowship society built around public benefit, mission affiliation, service, and recognition.

---

# Positioning Statement

US Fellows is the civic fellowship layer for public-interest talent.

It provides exceptional people with a recognized fellowship identity, connects them to mission-driven institutions, and supports their contribution to projects that serve humanity, civil society, science, and the nation.

# The US Fellows Standard

A US Fellow is selected not merely for achievement, but for the seriousness of their commitment to humanity-scale service.

US Fellows are individuals of talent, character, discipline, and public purpose. They may come from science, education, entrepreneurship, immigration, public policy, environmental work, civic innovation, research, technology, national-capacity fields, or other areas of public consequence.

What unites them is not a single profession.

What unites them is service.

---

# What Is a Fellow?

A US Fellow is an individual appointed by the Society to contribute to a public-interest mission through a structured fellowship affiliation.

Fellows may serve through NGO partnerships, research institutes, universities, civic institutions, public-interest organizations, mission-driven projects, or approved fellowship appointments.

Fellows are selected for their ability to contribute meaningfully to work that advances humanity, strengthens institutions, and serves the public good.

---

# Fellowship Model

US Fellows creates structured fellowship affiliations between exceptional people and mission-driven institutions.

Each fellowship appointment is designed around four elements:

1. **The Fellow**
   An individual selected for excellence, service, and mission alignment.

2. **The Mission**
   A public-interest area of meaningful consequence.

3. **The Host Institution**
   An NGO, research institute, university, civic institution, or public-interest organization supporting the fellowship appointment.

4. **The Contribution**
   A defined project, research effort, advisory role, civic initiative, field-aligned appointment, or public-benefit service commitment.

# Career Advancement Track

US Fellows’ Career Advancement Track helps national and international graduates pursue field-aligned public-interest fellowship appointments with qualified host organizations, research institutes, universities, and mission-driven institutions.

This track is designed for graduates seeking meaningful public-benefit work connected to their field of study, research background, professional interests, or civic mission.

The Career Advancement Track supports fellowship identity, host affiliation, project definition, public-interest contribution, and mission alignment.

---

# International Graduate Fellows

International Graduate Fellows are selected for their academic achievement, field alignment, service orientation, and commitment to public-interest work.

Through fellowship appointments with qualified host institutions, International Graduate Fellows contribute to research, civic projects, public-benefit initiatives, science, education, environmental work, humanitarian missions, and other field-aligned areas of public consequence.

US Fellows recognizes the extraordinary contribution international graduates can make to American institutions, research, innovation, civil society, and the public good.

---

# National Capacity Fellows

National Capacity Fellows are professionals whose expertise supports institutional strength, resilience, scientific capacity, infrastructure, civic systems, emergency preparedness, public-interest technology, and national service.

This fellowship exists for individuals who want to contribute their expertise to missions larger than themselves.

National Capacity Fellows may include professionals from research, defense-adjacent fields, cybersecurity, engineering, public systems, federal mission environments, infrastructure, emergency management, and other areas of national importance.

US Fellows does not exist to staff companies. It exists to recognize and mobilize service-minded expertise for public-interest missions.

---

# Host Institutions

Host Institutions are NGOs, research institutes, universities, civic institutions, public-interest organizations, mission-driven nonprofits, and approved entities that support fellowship appointments aligned with the public good.

A Host Institution provides the setting, supervision, mission context, and project framework through which a Fellow contributes meaningful service.

US Fellows works with Host Institutions to define mission opportunities, structure fellowship appointments, and ensure that each fellowship reflects the standards of the Society.

---

# Host Institution Summary

Host Institutions partner with US Fellows to receive exceptional Fellows into public-interest projects, research affiliations, civic initiatives, humanitarian work, environmental missions, educational efforts, and institutional service.

Each host relationship is built on mission alignment, public benefit, and respect for the Fellow’s contribution.

---

# Become a Host Institution

US Fellows invites NGOs, research institutes, universities, civic institutions, and mission-driven organizations to become Host Institutions for fellowship appointments.

Host Institutions may propose public-interest projects, research opportunities, civic initiatives, advisory needs, field-aligned graduate appointments, or mission-based service opportunities.

Approved Host Institutions become part of the US Fellows national fellowship ecosystem.

# Society Overview

US Fellows is more than a program.

It is a fellowship society.

The Society exists to honor service, convene exceptional people, elevate public-interest work, and create a lasting civic identity for Fellows committed to humanity-scale missions.

Through cohorts, chapters, convenings, recognition, alumni networks, advisors, and institutional partnerships, US Fellows builds a community of people dedicated to service beyond self.

---

# The Fellowship Society

The US Fellows Society is the civic home of Fellows, advisors, alumni, host institutions, and mission partners.

It convenes people across disciplines and generations who share a commitment to public service, institutional strength, scientific progress, human dignity, and the future of civil society.

The Society recognizes Fellows not only for what they have achieved, but for what they are willing to contribute.

---

# Code of Service

US Fellows are expected to act with integrity, humility, excellence, discipline, respect, and commitment to the public good.

Fellows serve missions, not ego.

Fellows strengthen institutions, not merely themselves.

Fellows honor the trust placed in them by the Society, their Host Institutions, and the communities their work may affect.

---

# Fellowship Oath

I accept the honor of fellowship in service to humanity, civil society, science, and the nation.

I will contribute my knowledge, discipline, imagination, and effort to missions larger than myself.

I will act with integrity, humility, and respect for the people and institutions I serve.

I will use my fellowship not merely as a distinction, but as a commitment.

I will honor the Society, strengthen the public good, and serve future generations.

---

# Eligibility

US Fellows welcomes exceptional people across disciplines who demonstrate talent, seriousness, integrity, and commitment to public-interest service.

Applicants may come from science, education, public policy, immigration, entrepreneurship, environmental work, technology, civic innovation, research, humanitarian service, national-capacity fields, or other areas of public consequence.

Selection is based on merit, mission alignment, public-benefit orientation, character, and the potential to contribute meaningfully to a fellowship appointment.

---

# Selection Criteria

US Fellows evaluates candidates according to:

* Excellence in their field or demonstrated potential for excellence
* Commitment to public benefit
* Alignment with humanity-scale missions
* Integrity and seriousness of purpose
* Ability to contribute to a Host Institution or mission project
* Evidence of service, leadership, creativity, research, advocacy, or civic contribution
* Respect for institutional responsibility and public trust

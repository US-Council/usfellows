The fellowship pathway

Professional growth aligned with public purpose

The Career Advancement Track helps graduates and emerging professionals build field-relevant experience through work that also serves an institutional or public need.

It is not generic placement. A sound appointment connects academic preparation, professional direction, host capacity, and a contribution with defined value.

Field relevance establishes the point of entry; public-interest setting defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

The Career Advancement Track helps graduates and emerging professionals build field-relevant experience through work that also serves an institutional or public need.

01

Field relevance

The work should use or deepen knowledge connected to the Fellow's education and intended profession.

02

Substantive scope

Responsibilities should produce credible experience and useful outputs, not observation alone.

03

Supported development

Hosts provide context, supervision, feedback, and exposure to responsible professional practice.

04

Public-interest setting

Growth occurs through research, civic, educational, scientific, humanitarian, or mission-driven work.

From intent to practice

From interest to appointment

Appointments are shaped deliberately so that recognition, institutional affiliation, and useful work reinforce one another.

Express interest

Describe your record, field, mission interests, and the contribution you are ready to make. Here, attend to field relevance: The work should use or deepen knowledge connected to the Fellow's education and intended profession.

Establish fit

Consider merit, readiness, mission alignment, and the setting in which the work can succeed. Here, attend to substantive scope: Responsibilities should produce credible experience and useful outputs, not observation alone.

Define the appointment

Confirm scope, host support, responsibilities, intended benefit, and a practical working cadence. Here, attend to supported development: Hosts provide context, supervision, feedback, and exposure to responsible professional practice.

Serve and reflect

Complete the work with rigor, participate in Society life, and share transferable learning. Here, attend to public-interest setting: Growth occurs through research, civic, educational, scientific, humanitarian, or mission-driven work.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

Field alignment

A Fellow's knowledge and capabilities should be relevant to the work the appointment requires.

02

Institutional fit

The host setting should make the contribution feasible, responsible, and useful.

03

Public value

The appointment should create benefit beyond private career advancement or organizational convenience.

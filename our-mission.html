Our purpose

Our Mission

US Fellows honors and connects exceptional people across disciplines to humanity-scale missions through public-interest projects, research affiliations, NGO partnerships, and service-oriented fellowships.

Mission in practice

The mission is intentionally broad enough to welcome excellence from many fields and disciplined enough to require a real public-interest contribution.

A framework for service of public consequence

US Fellows joins recognition to responsibility. Every affiliation should connect a capable person, a consequential mission, a trusted setting, and work that can be described plainly.

From intent to practice

How purpose becomes practice

The Society translates broad purpose into work that can be understood, supported, and evaluated.

Name the need

Define the public-interest challenge, its context, and the people or systems affected. Here, attend to recognize capability: Honor people whose work demonstrates substance, judgment, and the potential for further service.

Shape the contribution

Set a bounded role with clear responsibilities, useful outputs, and realistic scope. Here, attend to connect talent to mission: Help exceptional people locate institutions and problems where their abilities are genuinely relevant.

Support the work

Provide institutional context, supervision, access, and the conditions required for quality. Here, attend to structure contribution: Turn goodwill into a defined appointment with purpose, scope, support, and useful outputs.

Carry learning forward

Document outcomes and lessons so one contribution can strengthen the next. Here, attend to sustain civic identity: Create relationships and obligations that endure beyond a single project or period of service.

Put this work into practice

Begin with recognize capability, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

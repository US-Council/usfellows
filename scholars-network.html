Scholars Network

The Scholars Network connects people across every US Fellows R&D Scholar designation, career stage, institution, sector, and border. It turns a sequence of recognitions into an enduring community for mentorship, responsible collaboration, and humanity-scale research.

The connective tissue

One network, five forms of contribution

Scholar designations recognize different forms of readiness, responsibility, and public-benefit contribution. The network gives those designations a shared home. Members can remain in relationship as their work develops, without treating the designations as a guaranteed promotion ladder.

Its purpose is practical: help promising researchers find sound guidance, help established leaders form credible teams, help institutions discover trusted collaborators, and preserve the standards that make those relationships useful.

Recognition is not authorization

Network membership does not confer employment, funding, clearance, laboratory access, immigration status, contracting authority, or permission to speak for US Fellows or another institution. Every role and representation requires the appropriate separate approval.

Collaboration protocol

A good request is specific, evidenced, and permission-based

Scholars may contact a field-aligned scientist or network member for a bounded contribution. The recipient should not have to reconstruct the project's context before deciding whether the request merits attention.

Research context

Include the research question, objectives, institution, degree or program context, home advisor when applicable, and enough non-sensitive background to understand the work.

Evidence and permissions

Provide an accurate CV or record, relevant publications or work, advisor endorsement when available, and a truthful account of who has agreed to participate.

Opportunity and ask

Name the solicitation or collaboration opportunity, identify known eligibility constraints, outline proposed roles, and make one request that can be accepted, narrowed, referred, or declined.

For recipients

Participation is always discretionary

A recipient may advise, refer, explore a defined role, narrow the scope, ask staff to clarify, or decline. No one is obligated to supervise, endorse, join a proposal, provide laboratory access, accept legal responsibility, or help secure employment or funding.

A conversation or encouraging comment does not create supervision, authorship, PI or Key Personnel status, proposal participation, funding, employment, or institutional affiliation.

Before sharing or representing

Protect people, institutions, and information

  • Never send classified, controlled, proprietary, unpublished, export-restricted, or personally sensitive information through an informal network channel.
  • Follow employer rules for outside activity, conflicts, publications, data, intellectual property, and proposals.
  • Distinguish personal comments from institutionally authorized statements.
  • Obtain explicit permission before reusing a name, affiliation, endorsement, introduction, or proposed role.
Network standing

Trust must remain observable

Participation depends on accurate representation, respectful outreach, protection of sensitive information, compliance with law and institutional policy, and timely correction of errors. Scholar standing is governed by the shared standards and by any designation-specific continuing requirements.

Read standards and standing

No implied affiliation

A network introduction or request never authorizes anyone to list a recipient, laboratory, university, company, US Fellows, or another organization as a collaborator, supporter, sponsor, or participant. Silence is not approval; permission must be explicit and appropriately authorized.

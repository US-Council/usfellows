Code of Service

Fellows serve missions, not ego.

US Fellows are expected to act with integrity, humility, excellence, discipline, respect, and commitment to the public good.

Life in the Society

Conduct worthy of public trust

The Code of Service turns the Society's values into expectations for how Fellows approach institutions, communities, colleagues, knowledge, and recognition.

It applies in public and private conduct connected to fellowship and provides a common reference when circumstances are difficult or ambiguous.

Serve with integrity establishes the point of entry; use recognition responsibly defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

From intent to practice

How the Society stays active

The Society is sustained through recurring practices that turn affiliation into useful relationships and continued service.

01

Welcome

Orient Fellows to the standard, one another, and the missions that organize the Society's work. Attend specifically to serve with integrity.

02

Connect

Build trusted peer, advisor, host, and alumni relationships across disciplines and places. Attend specifically to respect people and institutions.

03

Convene

Create serious forums for learning, collaboration, reflection, and recognition of contribution. Attend specifically to practice disciplined humility.

04

Continue

Carry fellowship forward through mentorship, public-interest work, and future Society missions. Attend specifically to use recognition responsibly.

The substance

Four dimensions of the work

The Code of Service turns the Society&#x27;s values into expectations for how Fellows approach institutions, communities, colleagues, knowledge, and recognition.

01

Serve with integrity

Represent qualifications, evidence, affiliations, progress, and outcomes honestly.

02

Respect people and institutions

Honor dignity, consent, confidentiality, local knowledge, and responsible organizational processes.

03

Practice disciplined humility

Ask before assuming, acknowledge limits, accept feedback, and correct errors promptly.

04

Use recognition responsibly

Do not imply endorsement beyond an appointment or place personal visibility above the mission.

04

Put this work into practice

Begin with serve with integrity, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.

Becoming a Fellow

Excellence can emerge from any field

There is no single profession, career stage, geography, or academic pathway that defines a US Fellow.

Eligibility is broad by design, but selection is discerning. Candidates must show credible ability, integrity, mission relevance, and readiness for a structured public-interest affiliation.

Professionals and practitioners establishes the point of entry; senior and distinguished leaders defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.

The standard

Principles that hold the work

These tests connect public purpose to responsible practice.

01

A record with substance

Accomplishment may take many forms, but it should demonstrate capability, judgment, and sustained effort. In this context, consider professionals and practitioners.

02

Character under responsibility

Fellows are trusted with institutional relationships, communities, and the public meaning of the Society. In this context, consider researchers and scholars.

03

Readiness to contribute

Candidates should be able to explain what they can do, where it is relevant, and how they will approach service. In this context, consider graduates and emerging leaders.

From intent to practice

What the selection journey asks of you

The process is designed to understand the whole candidate: record, character, field, mission, and the practical contribution they are equipped to make.

Prepare

Gather a clear account of your work, capabilities, service, mission interests, and current readiness. Here, attend to professionals and practitioners: People with demonstrated capability in their field and a serious desire to direct it toward public purpose.

Apply or be nominated

Submit an expression of interest directly or through someone able to speak to your record and character. Here, attend to researchers and scholars: Individuals able to advance, translate, or apply knowledge within a mission and host context.

Review and conversation

The Society considers evidence, alignment, references where appropriate, and the conditions for a sound appointment. Here, attend to graduates and emerging leaders: Candidates with strong preparation, promise, judgment, and readiness for field-aligned contribution.

Appointment

Selected Fellows confirm expectations, host or project context, responsibilities, and the Code of Service. Here, attend to senior and distinguished leaders: Experienced people prepared to advise, mentor, convene, or lead work of major public consequence.

“Professionals and practitioners establishes the point of entry; senior and distinguished leaders defines what should remain after the immediate work is complete.”

Put this work into practice

Begin with professionals and practitioners, then define a contribution your capabilities or institution can support.
